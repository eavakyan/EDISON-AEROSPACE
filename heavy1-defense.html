Heavy 1 Defense

Unmanned Aerial Delivery & Munitions Platform

Home / Defense / Heavy 1 Defense

Multi-Domain Aerial Platform

The Heavy 1 Defense is a flying unmanned platform derived from Edison Aerospace's proven Heavy 1 airframe. While the UNCV handles maritime operations and the SeaWatch covers surface-effect missions, the Heavy 1 Defense provides the aerial component of Edison's multi-domain defense ecosystem.

It is designed for cargo delivery to forward positions and munitions deployment in contested airspace. With electric propulsion providing a low acoustic and thermal signature, the Heavy 1 Defense is inherently harder to detect than conventional platforms -- and its expendable economics make it a strategic force multiplier.

Heavy 1 Defense — 8 independent electric motors for redundancy and low acoustic signature

Technical Specifications

Wingspan 40 feet
Maximum Takeoff Weight (MTOW) 3,000 lbs
Payload 1,200+ lbs (modular cargo/munitions bay)
Takeoff Distance 300 feet (STOL)
Cruise Speed 90 knots
Powerplant 8 Electric Motors
Operations Autonomous, remotely piloted, or swarm-coordinated
Signature Low acoustic and thermal signature (electric)

Mission Profiles

Cargo Delivery

Resupply forward operating positions, deliver equipment and supplies to troops in contested or remote areas without risking pilot lives.

Munitions Deployment

Precision delivery of ordnance to designated targets. Loitering munition capability allows the platform to remain on station until the optimal engagement window.

Swarm Operations

Coordinate multiple Heavy 1 Defense units for distributed attack or saturation delivery. Overwhelm adversary defenses through simultaneous multi-axis engagement.

ISR Support

Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance with onboard sensors. Low-signature electric flight enables extended loiter time over areas of interest.

Decoy Operations

Serve as anti-aircraft decoys to protect manned assets. Expendable platform economics make it a cost-effective solution for saturating enemy air defenses.

CASEVAC Support

Emergency casualty evacuation from areas too dangerous for manned aircraft. Autonomous extraction reduces risk to rescue personnel in active combat zones.

Key Advantages

Low Signature

Electric propulsion produces minimal acoustic and thermal signature, making the Heavy 1 Defense inherently harder to detect by enemy sensors and air defenses.

No Pilot at Risk

Operate in contested airspace without risking human lives. The unmanned platform can execute high-risk missions that would be unacceptable for manned aircraft.

STOL Deployment

Deploy from damaged or improvised airstrips as short as 300 feet. No dependence on intact runway infrastructure in forward or contested areas.

Expendable Economics

Lower cost per unit than manned platforms enables attritable operations. Deploy as expendable assets in high-threat environments where platform loss is acceptable.

Battle Damage Tolerance

8-motor redundancy means the aircraft can sustain damage to multiple motors and continue its mission. No single point of failure in the propulsion system.

Multi-Domain Integration

Seamless integration with UNCV and SeaWatch for coordinated air, sea, and surface-effect operations across the full battlespace.
