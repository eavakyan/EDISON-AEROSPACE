Pilot Training

Transition to Electric Aviation

Home / Civilian / Pilot Training

Training Overview

Edison Aerospace offers a streamlined 10-hour transition training program for experienced agricultural pilots. The program is designed to get certified ag pilots flying the Heavy 1 quickly and confidently.

Our curriculum leverages the inherent simplicity of electric propulsion systems. With no complex turbine engine procedures, fuel management systems, or traditional prop governor operations to learn, experienced ag pilots can focus on mastering the unique advantages of the Heavy 1 platform.

Training Breakdown

A focused curriculum that gets you operational in just 10 hours.

10
Total Hours
4

Flight Hours

Classroom Training - 4 Hours

  • Electric propulsion systems overview and theory of operation
  • Battery management systems, charging procedures, and energy planning
  • Ground Control Station (GCS) operations and interface familiarization
  • Emergency procedures specific to electric aircraft systems
  • Regulatory requirements and FAA Part 137 compliance for electric aircraft
  • Pre-flight inspection procedures unique to the Heavy 1

Flight Training - 6 Hours

  • Aircraft familiarization flights and handling characteristics
  • Agricultural application patterns -- swath runs, ferry flights, turn procedures
  • Emergency procedures -- motor failure scenarios, forced landings, autorotation
  • GCS-assisted operations and autonomous flight mode transitions
  • Night operations familiarization (as applicable)
  • Solo operations and proficiency check

Training Location

Tioga, North Dakota

Edison Aerospace's training facility is located in Tioga, North Dakota -- in the heart of America's agricultural heartland. The location provides ideal conditions for agricultural aviation training, with vast open fields and real-world crop application environments.

Our FAA Part 137 compliant training program ensures pilots meet all federal requirements for agricultural aircraft operations. Training is conducted under realistic conditions that prepare pilots for the demands of commercial ag aviation.

Prerequisites

The Heavy 1 transition training program is designed for experienced agricultural pilots who meet the following requirements.

Agricultural Pilot Certificate

Current FAA agricultural aircraft operator certificate (Part 137) required.

Current Medical Certificate

Valid FAA medical certificate appropriate for the operations being conducted.

Recent Ag Flying Experience

Demonstrated recent experience in agricultural aviation operations.

Commercial Pilot License

Valid commercial pilot certificate with appropriate category and class ratings.
